RoboCare: Humanoid Robotics Tactile Upper Limb for Gentle Care and Intelligent Interaction

  • By Chan Wang Sing, Shilong Mu, Runze Zhao, Xinyue Chai
  • 2024
  • Tsinghua University

Global Elderly caregiver demand for household activities is expected to reach 65 million caregivers by 2030.[1] Exisiting humanoid robots, often lacking advanced tactile sensing, fall short in performing delicate tasks essential for caregiving. RoboCare addresses the growing caregiver demand by introducing a tactile robotic upper limb for humanoid robots. Featuring a rigid-soft hybrid arm with airbags for safe interaction and a dexterous hand with high-resolution tactile sensors A unified control strategy seamlessly integrates these components, enabling the robot to perform complex tasks such as pick-and-place operations, handovers, and fine manipulations. By advancing tactile robotics, RoboCare aims to offer intelligent and sensitive robotic assistance for caregiving and household tasks, thereby enhancing the quality of life as we approach 2030.

[1] Goldman Sachs Research, Humanoid robot: The AI accelerant (2024)
